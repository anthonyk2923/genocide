Be an upstander!

As an upstander, it is essential to recognize what it takes to stop a genocide. There are many things a citizen can do to prevent injustices from happening. It involves speaking up and taking action to promote equality and peace. One of the things a person can do is spread word through the media. This can include posting about it on social media or recording a video for others to have tape evidence of it. It is a powerful tool that changes things for the better and encourages others to take action. To be an upstander you have to do something not because it’s heroic but for the reason that anyone else would do the same thing if they were in your shoes. A very signification group of upstanders seen in the past was the resistance movement during the Holocaust. They fought back against the Nazis in many concentration camps and rescued many Jews. As Jane Springer mentions in her book, Genocide, “Holocaust determined that the individuals helping stop the genocide had these characteristics: “a strong sense of individuality, self-reliance, a history of standing up for others, and a tendency to see their aid as ordinary, and not particularly heroic” (105, Springer). These are just some of what a person needs to possess, in general, they only need one of these. Nonetheless, it takes one person to speak up and start a domino effect around the world. We urge you to be an upstander in times of injustice, initiate peaceful protests, raise awareness, contact relative authorities, and organizations.
